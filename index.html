autumn poem

An autumn poem

The leaves fall, as from afar, as if withered in heaven's remote gardens; it is with reluctance that they fall.
And during the nights weighty earth falls from all the stars into solitude.

All of us fall. This hand falls here. And look at others: All of them fall.
But there is One, Who holds what falls with infinite tenderness in His hands.
